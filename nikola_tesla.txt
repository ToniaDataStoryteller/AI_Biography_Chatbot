Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor born during a lightning storm in 1856.
He is best known for designing the modern alternating current or AC electricity system.
Tesla moved to the United States in 1884 to work for Thomas Edison in New York.
Thomas Edison was his boss before they became rivals in the famous War of Currents.
Edison supported direct current power while Tesla promoted alternating current power.
Tesla's first major invention was the induction motor which used magnetic fields.
The Tesla Coil was invented in 1891 and is still used in radio technology today.
Tesla and George Westinghouse worked together to light the Chicago World Fair in 1893.
He designed the first hydroelectric power plant at Niagara Falls to generate electricity.
Tesla claimed he could visualize entire inventions in his mind without using drawings.
He had a photographic memory and could speak eight different languages fluently.
Tesla died in 1943 in a hotel room in New York City after a long career of innovation.
